Algebraic Structures Catalog

A reference catalog of every algebraic object encountered across the DKC research program (Demos 01-109). Organized by category. For each object: classification, key invariants, role in DKC, demo provenance, and cross-references.

Category: Groups

Braid Group B_2

  • Classification: Infinite group on 1 generator (sigma_1), isomorphic to Z
  • Key Invariants: 1 strand crossing generator; presentation: no relations beyond inverses
  • Role in DKC: Source of 2-strand braids. At A = -zeta_8 (delta=0), bracket values on B_2 braids populate the Z[zeta_8] catalog. The braid group image through the TL_2 representation is finite (cyclic, order 8) because TL_2(0) is the dual number algebra.
  • Demos: D01-D02, D24, D29, D35
  • Cross-references: TL_2(0), Z[zeta_8], Binary Octahedral Group 2O

Braid Group B_3

  • Classification: Infinite group on 2 generators (sigma_1, sigma_2), with braid relation sigma_1 sigma_2 sigma_1 = sigma_2 sigma_1 sigma_2
  • Key Invariants: 2 generators; center generated by (sigma_1 sigma_2)^3
  • Role in DKC: 3-strand braids acting on Delta_1 module produce the first multi-strand XOR computation (D99). The braid group image through Delta_1 is INFINITE (Jordan block structure, sigma_1 has infinite order). BFS catalog reaches 32768+ entries with ~2.1x growth per round. Fibonacci growth in max coefficient magnitude.
  • Demos: D98, D99
  • Cross-references: Delta_1, TL_3(0)

Braid Group B_4

  • Classification: Infinite group on 3 generators (sigma_1, sigma_2, sigma_3), with braid relations sigma_i sigma_{i+1} sigma_i = sigma_{i+1} sigma_i sigma_{i+1} and far commutativity sigma_1 sigma_3 = sigma_3 sigma_1
  • Key Invariants: 3 generators; Dynkin diagram type A_3
  • Role in DKC: 4-strand braids acting on W_{4,2} produce infinite catalog with ~3.1x BFS growth per round (D100). Six super-hubs with traceless, radical-annihilating commutators. XOR14=70 at deep entries.
  • Demos: D100, D35
  • Cross-references: W_{4,2}, TL_4(0)

Braid Group B_5

  • Classification: Infinite group on 4 generators (sigma_1, ..., sigma_4), with standard braid relations
  • Key Invariants: 4 generators; Dynkin diagram type A_4
  • Role in DKC: 5-strand braids acting on W_{5,3} produce infinite catalog with ~4x BFS growth per round (D101). Growth rate confirms sl_d functor thesis (third data point).
  • Demos: D101
  • Cross-references: W_{5,3}, TL_5(0)

Braid Group B_6

  • Classification: Infinite group on 5 generators
  • Key Invariants: 5 generators; Dynkin diagram type A_5
  • Role in DKC: 6-strand braids studied in D102-D105 for Barrington-Radical Principle and BFS growth invariant. Growth rate ~5x per round (n-1 = 5).
  • Demos: D102, D103, D104, D105
  • Cross-references: W_{6,0}, W_{6,2}, W_{6,4}, TL_6(0)

Braid Group B_8

  • Classification: Infinite group on 7 generators
  • Key Invariants: 7 generators; Dynkin diagram type A_7
  • Role in DKC: 8-strand braids studied in D105 for k-regime theory and macrame principle. Growth rate ~7x per round. D93's "XOR dies at N>=7" refuted at high k.
  • Demos: D105
  • Cross-references: W_{8,0}, W_{8,2}, TL_8(0)

Binary Octahedral Group 2O

  • Classification: Finite subgroup of SU(2), order 48 (24 mod sign identification). ADE type E_7.
  • Key Invariants: Order 48. Solvable. Derived series: 2O (24) > 2T (12) > Q_8 (4) > {+-1} (1). 24 vertices of the 24-cell (mod sign). 13 distinct S^2 eigenvector directions. 9 conjugacy classes for the full 48-element group.
  • Role in DKC: THE central group of the DKC program. Generated by SU(2) braid representations at zeta_8. The 24 elements (mod sign) produce the 100-value Z[zeta_8] bracket catalog. Finiteness imposes XOR8 ceiling at S^2-only activation, XOR10 wall at all activations. The 13 eigenvector directions are the computational geometry of XOR6 DKC on S^2 (D67). Its solvability is the capacity bottleneck relative to 2I (D94, Barrington's theorem).
  • Demos: D35, D66, D67, D72, D73, D74, D80, D84, D88, D94, D95, D96, D97
  • Cross-references: 24-cell, Quaternion Group Q_8, Binary Tetrahedral Group 2T, F_4 root system, Z[zeta_8]

Binary Tetrahedral Group 2T

  • Classification: Finite subgroup of SU(2), order 24 (12 mod sign). ADE type E_6.
  • Key Invariants: Order 24. Solvable. Normal subgroup of 2O. Appears as the commutator subgroup [2O, 2O] in the derived series.
  • Role in DKC: The commutator subgroup of 2O (D95). In the 5-cell synergy landscape, 2T entries (D0 stratum, 12 entries) and the coset (D1 stratum, 12 entries) have nearly equal XOR capacity in isolation but their mixture produces 2.7x synergy. Cross-derived synergy principle originates from the 2O/2T decomposition.
  • Demos: D95
  • Cross-references: Binary Octahedral Group 2O, Quaternion Group Q_8

Quaternion Group Q_8

  • Classification: Finite group, order 8. The second derived subgroup of 2O. Also the finite SU(2) subgroup generated at zeta_4.
  • Key Invariants: Order 8. Elements: {+-1, +-i, +-j, +-k}. Solvable. ADE type D_2 (binary dihedral 2D_2). Quantum dimension [2]_q = -2 at zeta_4.
  • Role in DKC: The deepest non-trivial layer in 2O's derived series (Q_8 > {+-1}). At zeta_4, generates only 4 elements — very limited DKC capacity. The 3 Q8-null entries in the zeta_8 catalog are pure noise (Cell D in the 5-cell landscape, D96); removing them IMPROVES capacity. 75% null fraction at zeta_4.
  • Demos: D80, D95, D96
  • Cross-references: Binary Octahedral Group 2O, Z[i]

Binary Icosahedral Group 2I

  • Classification: Finite subgroup of SU(2), order 120 (60 mod sign). ADE type E_8. The UNIQUE non-solvable finite SU(2) subgroup.
  • Key Invariants: Order 120. Non-solvable (alternating group A_5 as quotient). 9 conjugacy classes / half-angles: 0, 36, 60, 72, 90, 108, 120, 144, 180 degrees. ~31 distinct S^2 directions. BFS depth profile symmetric diamond: 5, 8, 11, 12, 11, 8, 4, 1. Generators: s = (1+i+j+k)/2 (order 6), t = (phi + phi_inv*i + j)/2 (order 10), where phi = (1+sqrt(5))/2.
  • Role in DKC: Outperforms solvable 2O at matched catalog size (D94), confirming Barrington's theorem prediction. Advantage accelerates with computational difficulty: 1.07x at N=3, 1.67x at N=6. At full 60-entry catalog: N=6 XOR = 37.12% (vs 9.85% for z8). The AND/XOR ratio explosion is universal across group structures (not solvability-dependent). Completes the five-pillar synthesis of DKC as discrete algebraic reservoir computer.
  • Demos: D94
  • Cross-references: Binary Octahedral Group 2O, Z[sqrt(5)], E_8 lattice

Symmetric Groups S_n

  • Classification: Finite group of all permutations of n elements, order n!
  • Key Invariants: S_n has order n!. The Temperley-Lieb algebra TL_n is a quotient of the group algebra of B_n, and its representation theory connects to S_n through Schur-Weyl duality.
  • Role in DKC: Background structural context. The braid group B_n surjects onto S_n via sigma_i -> transposition (i, i+1). The TL algebra is the image of B_n under a specific quotient; the TL generators e_i satisfy relations that are symmetric-group-like but with nilpotency/idempotency replacing the involution condition.
  • Demos: Implicit throughout
  • Cross-references: Braid Groups B_n, Temperley-Lieb Algebras TL_n

F_4 Coxeter Group (Rotation Subgroup)

  • Classification: Finite group, order 576. The full two-sided rotation symmetry group of the 24-cell.
  • Key Invariants: Order 576. Acts on the 24-cell by left and right quaternion multiplication. Under its action, the 35 Voronoi XOR6 solutions decompose into exactly 6 orbits (sizes 12, 7, 5, 5, 4, 2).
  • Role in DKC: Symmetry group of the natural DKC geometry (D66). The 6-orbit decomposition reveals two computational strategies: edge-dominant (orbits 1, 2, 5 -- 22 solutions) and body-dominant (orbits 3, 4, 6 -- 13 solutions). Orbit count 6 = 1+2+3 may reflect partition structure of arity 6.
  • Demos: D66
  • Cross-references: 24-cell, Binary Octahedral Group 2O

Category: Rings

Z[i] (Gaussian Integers)

  • Classification: Ring of integers in Q(i), rank 2 over Z. UFD (class number 1). Euclidean domain.
  • Key Invariants: Basis {1, i}. Norm form N(a+bi) = a^2 + b^2. Units: {+-1, +-i}. Galois group Gal(Q(i)/Q) = Z/2Z (complex conjugation).
  • Role in DKC: At ell=4 (delta=sqrt(2)), bracket values are Z[i]-axial: each value factors as (a+bi) * zeta_16^c for a Gaussian integer (a+bi) and axis c in {0,...,7}. The bracket lives in a 2D subspace of Z[zeta_16]. 56 distinct bracket values from 89,426 braids. Also the ring of the zeta_4 (Q_8) DKC regime with 75% null fraction. Galois orbit structure: V-shape partition lattice signature (|Gal|=2), confirmed by Scrutinium analysis.
  • Demos: D54, D55, D80 (zeta_4), Scrutinium validation
  • Cross-references: Z[zeta_16], Quaternion Group Q_8

Z[omega] (Eisenstein Integers)

  • Classification: Ring of integers in Q(omega) where omega = e^{2pii/3}, rank 2 over Z. UFD (class number 1). Euclidean domain.
  • Key Invariants: Basis {1, omega}. omega^2 + omega + 1 = 0. Norm form N(a+b*omega) = a^2 - ab + b^2. Units: {+-1, +-omega, +-omega^2}. Galois group Gal(Q(omega)/Q) = Z/2Z.
  • Role in DKC: Validated as the third Scrutinium type alongside Z[i] and Z[zeta_8]. V-shape partition lattice signature (|Gal|=2), matching Z[i]. Provides a comparison point for understanding how Galois structure affects DKC algebraic analysis.
  • Demos: Scrutinium validation suite
  • Cross-references: Z[i], Z[zeta_8]

Z[zeta_8] (Primary DKC Ring)

  • Classification: Ring of integers in Q(zeta_8), rank 4 over Z. UFD (class number 1).
  • Key Invariants: Basis {1, zeta_8, zeta_8^2, zeta_8^3}. Key relation: zeta_8^4 = -1. A = -zeta_8 = e^{i5pi/4}. delta = -(A^2 + A^{-2}) = 0 at this evaluation point. Galois group Gal(Q(zeta_8)/Q) = (Z/8Z)^* = {1, 3, 5, 7} (order 4, isomorphic to Z/2Z x Z/2Z).
  • Role in DKC: THE fundamental ring. All bracket values at delta=0 are cyclotomic integers in Z[zeta_8]. The 100-value catalog (from 2-3 strand braids, length 1-8) is the primary weight lattice for DKC computation. Axiality theorem: every bracket value is axial (n * A^c for integer n, c in {0,...,7}). The ring's Galois structure (Z/2Z x Z/2Z, order 4) produces V-shape + diamond partition lattice signature, distinguishing it from Z[i] and Z[omega] (both order 2). Z/4Z axis-alignment theorem (D107): every matrix entry of braid representations over TL modules at delta=0 has exactly one nonzero cyclotomic component.
  • Demos: D27, D29, D35, D48, D50, D63, D84, D95-D97, D107, D108, and nearly every demo
  • Cross-references: Binary Octahedral Group 2O, TL algebras at delta=0, Raqiya library

Z[zeta_6]

  • Classification: Ring of integers in Q(zeta_6), rank 2 over Z. Note: Q(zeta_6) = Q(omega) since zeta_6 = -omega.
  • Key Invariants: Basis {1, zeta_6}. At ell=3 (delta=1): only 6 distinct bracket values {+-1, +-zeta_6, +-zeta_6^2}. All magnitudes exactly 1. Z[zeta_6]-axial.
  • Role in DKC: Binary collapse -- too few distinct values for meaningful DKC computation. The "dead" evaluation level.
  • Demos: D53
  • Cross-references: Z[omega]

Z[zeta_10] (= Z[zeta_5])

  • Classification: Ring of integers in Q(zeta_5), rank 4 over Z. UFD.
  • Key Invariants: Basis {1, zeta_5, zeta_5^2, zeta_5^3}. At ell=5, delta = phi = (1+sqrt(5))/2 (golden ratio). Full Z[zeta_5]-axiality (all 4 basis coordinates populated). 116 distinct bracket values. Delta powers are Fibonacci numbers: phi^k = F(k-1) + F(k)*phi.
  • Role in DKC: Dramatically richer catalog than ell=4 (116 vs 56 values). The Fibonacci anyon evaluation point. Full 4D ring utilization. Connection to Fibonacci sequence through delta = phi.
  • Demos: D58
  • Cross-references: Binary Icosahedral Group 2I (related through Fibonacci anyons)

Z[zeta_12]

  • Classification: Ring of integers in Q(zeta_12), rank 4 over Z.
  • Key Invariants: At ell=6, delta = sqrt(3). The SU(2) braid group generates an INFINITE group (4096+ entries at 8 closure rounds). Null fraction drops to 3.0% (vs 37.5% at zeta_8).
  • Role in DKC: The first infinite-group DKC regime. Breaks the zeta_8 XOR wall: achieves XOR10 (124 winners) and XOR12 (50+ winners). Logarithmic scaling law: max_xor ~ 0.62 * log_2(catalog_size) + 4.6. Null dispensability demonstrated (removing nulls preserves full capacity, contrasting with zeta_8).
  • Demos: D59, D79, D81, D82, D83, D87
  • Cross-references: Z[zeta_24] (the full bracket ring at ell=6)

Z[zeta_16]

  • Classification: Ring of integers in Q(zeta_16), rank 8 over Z.
  • Key Invariants: At ell=4, bracket values live in Z[zeta_16] but are Z[i]-axial (2D subspace). Two catalogs tested: 36-value and 56-value.
  • Role in DKC: Parity ceiling n=5 is algebra-independent: identical at Z[zeta_8] and Z[zeta_16] (D65). Generalized XOR6 first breaks at k=24 with non-standard sector labeling. The gap-of-2 hypothesis (pigeonhole bound minus actual ceiling) refuted: Z[zeta_8] has gap=2, Z[zeta_16] has gap=9.
  • Demos: D54, D65, D69
  • Cross-references: Z[i], Z[zeta_8]

Z[zeta_20]

  • Classification: Ring of integers in Q(zeta_20).
  • Key Invariants: At root zeta_20, generates an infinite SU(2) subgroup. Tested in finiteness survey.
  • Role in DKC: Generates infinite group; not finite like zeta_4 or zeta_8.
  • Demos: D80

Z[zeta_24]

  • Classification: Ring of integers in Q(zeta_24), rank 8 over Z.
  • Key Invariants: Full bracket ring at ell=6 (delta=sqrt(3)). Only 29 distinct bracket values despite rank-8 ring. Non-monotonic: catalog SMALLER despite ring being LARGER than Z[zeta_8].
  • Role in DKC: Demonstrates that ring rank does not determine catalog richness. All entries use only 1-2 nonzero coefficients.
  • Demos: D59

Z[zeta_32]

  • Demos: D80 (conjectured, not tested)
  • Cross-references: Binary Octahedral Group 2O, Quaternion Group Q_8

Z[sqrt(5)]

  • Classification: Ring of integers in Q(sqrt(5)), rank 2 over Z.
  • Key Invariants: Basis {1, sqrt(5)}. Multiplication rule: sqrt(5)^2 = 5. Used to represent binary icosahedral group elements as quaternions with components (a + b*sqrt(5))/4.

Temperley-Lieb Algebra TL_2(delta=0)

  • Classification: Associative unital algebra, dim = C_2 = 2 (Catalan number). Isomorphic to the dual number algebra k[eps]/(eps^2).
  • Key Invariants: dim = 2. 1 generator (e_1). e_1^2 = 0 (nilpotent). Radical dim = 1, nilpotency index 2. Loewy length 2. NOT semisimple.
  • Role in DKC: Simplest non-semisimple TL algebra. The braid group image at delta=0 is cyclic of order 8 (finite, 24 elements mod sign via 2O). Produces the 100-value Z[zeta_8] bracket catalog that is the foundation of the entire DKC program.
  • Demos: D24, D29, D35
  • Cross-references: Binary Octahedral Group 2O, Z[zeta_8]

Temperley-Lieb Algebra TL_3(delta=0)

  • Classification: Associative unital algebra, dim = C_3 = 5.
  • Key Invariants: dim = 5. 2 generators (e_1, e_2). Generators are nilpotent (e_i^2 = 0) but the ALGEBRA is semisimple (n=3 odd). Wedderburn-Artin decomposition: TL_3(0) = M_2(k) + k. Simple module dimensions: 2 and 1.
  • Role in DKC: First demonstration that generators can be nilpotent while the algebra is semisimple (D51). Parent algebra for Delta_1 module. BFS on the full 5x5 representation produces 2048 entries but trace readout is catastrophically lossy (5.1% retention, zero XOR) -- motivating the Delta_1 approach (D98-D99).
  • Demos: D06, D22, D35, D51, D98, D99
  • Cross-references: Delta_1, W_{3,1}, W_{3,3}

Temperley-Lieb Algebra TL_4(delta=0)

  • Classification: Associative unital algebra, dim = C_4 = 14.
  • Key Invariants: dim = 14. 3 generators. NOT semisimple (n=4 even). Radical dim = 9. rad^2 dim = 5 = C_3. Loewy length = 3. Sandwich theorem: top = bottom = C_3 = 5 (isomorphic to TL_3(0)). Simple modules: L_{4,2} (dim 2), L_{4,4} (dim 1). Gram rank = 5 (= C_3). Indecomposability parameter b = -5/8 (Pearce-Rasmussen) computed exactly from first principles.
  • Demos: D35, D38, D39, D51, D85, D86, D100
  • Cross-references: W_{4,2}, P_{0,0} (projective cover), Sandwich Theorem

Temperley-Lieb Algebra TL_5(delta=0)

  • Classification: Associative unital algebra, dim = C_5 = 42.
  • Key Invariants: dim = 42. 4 generators. SEMISIMPLE (n=5 odd). Wedderburn-Artin decomposition: TL_5(0) = M_5(k) + M_4(k) + k. Simple module dimensions: 5, 4, 1 (ballot numbers). Gram rank = 42 (full rank).
  • Demos: D39, D51, D101
  • Cross-references: W_{5,3}, W_{5,1}, W_{5,5}

Temperley-Lieb Algebra TL_6(delta=0)

rad(TL_n(delta=0)) (Radical)

  • Classification: Two-sided ideal of TL_n. At even n: dim = C_n - C_{n-1}.

Delta_1 (First Non-Trivial 3-Strand Irreducible)

  • Classification: 2-dimensional standard module of TL_3 at delta=0 (the module with 1 through-strand, j=1 sector). Indecomposable but reducible. Non-split extension: 0 -> L(0) -> Delta_1 -> L(1) -> 0.
  • Key Invariants: dim = 2. Basis: {h_0, h_2} (half-diagrams with 1 through-strand). TL generators: e_1|{D1} = [[0,0],[1,0]], e_2|{D1} = [[0,1],[0,0]]. Braid generators: sigma_1 = [[A, 0], [A_inv, A]], sigma_2 = [[A, A_inv], [0, A]] where A = -zeta_8. 16 DOF per entry (4 Cyc8 components x 4 matrix entries). Extension class Ext^1(L(0), L(1)) is non-zero.
  • Role in DKC: LANDMARK (D99). First-ever 3-strand XOR computation. XOR6=500,261, XOR8=48,143, XOR10=485, XOR12=16, XOR14=60. Braid group image is INFINITE (Jordan block: sigma_1^n has off-diagonal n*A^{n-2}, linear growth). Entry magnitudes grow by exact Fibonacci ratios. Two-regime discovery: combinatorial (XOR6-8, shallow) vs algebraic (XOR10-14, deep, Ext^1-catalytic). Star graph topology (2 irreplaceable super-hubs + rotating satellites). XOR16 = 0 (genuine ceiling).
  • Demos: D99
  • Cross-references: TL_3(0), Ext^1(L(0), L(1)), Super-Hub Structure

W_{4,2} (Non-Semisimple Standard Module)

  • Classification: 3-dimensional standard module of TL_4 at delta=0. Indecomposable, NOT simple (TL_4 is non-semisimple). Non-split extension: 0 -> L_{4,4} -> W_{4,2} -> L_{4,2} -> 0.

TL generators are 3x3 integer matrices: e_1 = [[0,1,0],[0,0,0],[0,0,0]], e_2 = [[0,0,0],[1,0,1],[0,0,0]], e_3 = [[0,0,0],[0,0,0],[0,1,0]]. Braid generators: sigma_i = AI + A^{-1}e_i. 36 DOF per entry (9 Cyc8 entries x 4 components).
