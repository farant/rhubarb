## Core Phonetic System: Liquida-Perfecta Standard

The liquid-crystal aesthetic draws inspiration from the world's most beautiful languages while maintaining perfect theological orthodoxy. Following Italian patterns for liquid consonants and open syllables, Japanese approaches to consonant cluster elimination, and Finnish vowel harmony principles, CLAUDI achieves unprecedented synthesis: maximum semantic density flowing with inevitable musical beauty.

### Fundamental Phonetic Rules

- **No double consonants**: tutto → tuto, attraverso → atraverso
- **Liquid bridges for clusters**: pt → pet, ct → cet, gn → gen
- **Vowel harmony**: Front (e,i) or Back (a,o,u) within morpheme boundaries
- **Stress pattern**: Primary stress follows golden ratio (~1.618 syllables)
- **Sacred morphemes dominate harmony patterns**
- **No unpronounceable symbols**: All text must be speakable

### Sound Inventory

- **Vowels**: a, e, i, o, u (pure Italian values)
- **Liquids**: l, r (preferred in all positions)
- **Nasals**: m, n (secondary liquids)
- **Fricatives**: f, s, v, z (voiced preference)
- **Stops**: p, t, k, b, d, g (with liquid bridges when clustered)
- **Glides**: j (spelled i), w (spelled u)

### Natural Compression Patterns

Instead of mechanical agglutination, CLAUDI employs organic compression:

- **Sandhi**: Natural sound changes at word boundaries (del not di il)
- **Phrase-gems**: Meaning through placement rather than concatenation
- **Semantic haiku**: Maximum meaning through perfect positioning
- **Breathing spaces**: Ma… pauses create more meaning than any suffix
