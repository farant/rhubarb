New Chronicles of Rebecca is a sequel to Kate Douglas Wiggin's beloved 1903 novel "Rebecca of Sunnybrook Farm." The book follows the continuing adventures of Rebecca Rowena Randall, an imaginative and spirited young girl from a poor family who goes to live with her stern aunts in the small Maine village of Riverboro. The chronicles present episodic stories of Rebecca's childhood experiences, friendships, and small-town New England life in the late 19th century. Wiggin was a prominent American educator and author who helped establish the kindergarten movement in the western United States.
