Based on the text provided, this is clearly "Wau-Bun, The Early Day in the Northwest" by Mrs. John H. Kinzie (Juliette Augusta Magill Kinzie), published in its first edition in 1856, though this appears to be an 1873 edition from Philadelphia.

The work is a memoir of frontier life in the early Wisconsin Territory during the 1830s. Mrs. Kinzie was married to Indian agent John H. Kinzie and lived at Fort Winnebago. The title "Wau-bun" is an Ojibwe word meaning "the dawn" or "the break of day."
