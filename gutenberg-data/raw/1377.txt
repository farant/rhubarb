The Talisman is a historical novel set during the Third Crusade (1189-1192), featuring Richard the Lionheart of England and the Kurdish Muslim leader Saladin. The story follows a Scottish knight, Sir Kenneth, who becomes entangled in the political intrigues among the Crusader leaders while a mysterious physician with connections to Saladin plays a pivotal role. The novel explores themes of chivalry, honor, religious conflict, and the contrast between Eastern and Western cultures. It was published as the second part of Scott's "Tales of the Crusaders" series, paired with "The Betrothed."
