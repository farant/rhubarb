Hakluyt (c. 1552-1616) was an English writer, geographer, and Anglican clergyman who compiled extensive accounts of English exploration and trade voyages. This collection preserves primary source narratives of English maritime expeditions, trade missions, and encounters with African peoples and territories during the age of exploration. The work is invaluable for understanding Elizabethan-era English overseas expansion, early modern geography, and the beginnings of English colonial enterprise. This edition was later edited by Edmund Goldsmid in the 19th century.
