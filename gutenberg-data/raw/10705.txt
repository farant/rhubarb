This is the fifth and final volume of Theodor Mommsen's monumental "Römische Geschichte" (The History of Rome), which covers the period of the late Roman Republic and the establishment of the Roman Empire under Julius Caesar and Augustus. Mommsen (1817-1903) was a German classical scholar who won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1902, primarily for this groundbreaking historical work. Book V specifically examines the transformation from the Roman Republic to the "military monarchy" - the period of civil wars, the rise of powerful military commanders like Caesar and Pompey, and the eventual establishment of the Imperial system. The work is notable for its detailed analysis of Roman political, social, and economic institutions, written with both scholarly rigor and literary excellence. This English translation by William Purdie Dickson made the work accessible to English-speaking audiences.
