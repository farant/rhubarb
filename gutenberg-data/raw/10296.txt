The Fisk Jubilee Singers first toured in 1871, introducing spirituals to a wider American and European audience and raising funds for Fisk University, a historically black university in Nashville, Tennessee founded shortly after the Civil War. These spirituals emerged from the enslaved African-American experience, carrying both coded messages of escape and profound expressions of Christian faith and hope. "Swing Low, Sweet Chariot" specifically uses the imagery of Elijah being taken to heaven in a chariot as a metaphor for spiritual deliverance and, in some interpretations, escape from slavery via the Underground Railroad.
