David Hume's monumental "History of England" is a comprehensive historical work covering English history from the Roman invasion under Julius Caesar through the Glorious Revolution of 1688. Volume I covers the early period of English history. Published in multiple volumes between 1754 and 1762, this work was one of the most important historical texts of the 18th century and remained a standard reference for over a century. Hume, a Scottish Enlightenment philosopher and historian, approached English history with a philosophical and analytical perspective, attempting to apply reason and skepticism to historical analysis. His work was notable for its literary style and its attempt to explain historical events through natural causes rather than divine providence. The work was controversial in its time for its treatment of religious conflicts and for what some saw as Hume's sympathies toward the Stuart monarchs.
